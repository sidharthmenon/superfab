Fab Lab Kerala

A Fab Lab (Fabrication Laboratory) is a technical prototyping platform where people can design and build their own products. Since beginning as an outreach program from MIT‘s Center for Bits and Atoms (CBA), the number of Fab Labs across the world are doubling every two years as institutions across the world are using Fab labs as a stimulus for promoting local entrepreneurship and to serve as a platform for learning and innovation.

With the support of the Government of Kerala, Kerala Startup Mission has setup Fab labs in association with the Center for Bits & Atoms, MIT, USA at Technopark, Trivandrum and Technology Innovation Zone, Kochi. Fab Lab Kerala offers design and fabrication services and training and also gives Fab Academy graduates access to advanced machines for prototyping.

OMAX 5555

The abrasive water jet machining center to cut almost any material

OMAX 5555

The Abrasive water jet cutter is unlike any conventional machine. It compose of a high pressure water system, accelerating water through a precision nozzle at near sonic speeds. The water carries with it an abrasive particle which chips away the material, thereby cutting it with precision. The water jet is a dynamic tool that can cut complex shapes in a wide range of materials from simple metals to complex composites. It plays an integral part in creating the structure for machines that can be built in the lab. It can be used to cut parts like metal gears, sprockets, machine frame machine parts etc.

High performance wire cut machine for making molds, die and gears

Wire EDM

Wire EDMs are used for making parts and gears, cutting hardened metals and steels into very precise and small parts. This capability is one of the core competencies for machine building. The Wire-EDM cuts using a thin brass wire a little larger than a hair in diameter. The machine electrifies the brass wire, creating a spark between the wire and the workpiece, eroding a small amount of material. This process is repeated over and over again at a high frequency to cut very precise 2D paths in any kind of metal. The Wire-EDM machine is capable of cutting metals over 6-inches in thickness with a cut width less than 0.25mm.

MIT CBA

MIT's Center for Bits and Atoms is an interdisciplinary initiative exploring the boundary between computer science and physical science. CBA studies how to turn data into things, and things into data.

CBA was launched by a National Science Foundation award in 2001 to create a unique digital fabrication facility that gathers tools across disciplines and length scales for making and measuring things.

CBA personnel have participated in advances at the boundary between bits and atoms including what were among the first complete quantum computations, using nuclear spins in molecules; physical one-way cryptographic functions, implemented by mesoscopic light scattering; microfluidic bubble logic, with bits that transport materials as well as information; asynchronous logic automata, to align hardware with software; intelligent infrastructure, for energy efficiency; recoding the genome; coded folding, for programming matter; and the additive assembly of functional digital materials.
